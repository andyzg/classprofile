Academics

Grades

Starting in 1B, class average gradually increased over time. We can also notice that B terms tend to yield a slighly lower average, this is likely the results of hard courses being taken on a B term, such as ECE 106, ECE 124, SE 380, etc.

4A and 4B term average appear to be significantly higher among respondents. This can be due to several reasons; starting in 4th year SE students are able to pick 3-4 elective per term, it may as well be the result of the transition to an online delivery for courses or because only 19 respondents disclosed their average in their 4B term.

Grades from 1A to 4A

Attendance

Attendance consistently drops term by term, except after 3B This could be because students enjoyed their 4A & 4B classes more, or maybe students enjoyed attending courses asynchronously.

Attendance

Failures

34% of respondents failed at least one midterm and 14.5% of respondents failed at least one course. Failing a midterm does not necessarily mean that the course will be failed as well.

Failures

Options

2 respondents completed an option. Graduating with an Option can be complicated to do in Engineering. With a restrictive amount of electives, most students require to either overload a term or terms, or complete courses over coop in order to meet all the requirements for the Option and for graduating in SE.

Favourite Professors

The most favourite professor with 14% of the votes is Martin Pei. For this section, students were only able to select one professor among all the professors they had during their academic career.

Some professors listed in this section did not teach a core course, which means not every student had the opportunity to be taught with said professors.

Courses

Favourite courses

Among core courses, MATH 239 (Introduction to Combinatorics) and CS 349 (User Interface) were the most popular courses. Among electives ECE 454 (Distributed Computing) was the most popular. The top favourited courses were all taken between 3A and 4B, this can either be because students don't remember much of their first and second year courses, or that students enjoyed more higher level courses.

Favourite mandatory courses

Least favourite required courses

SE 463 (Software Requirements Specification and Analysis), ECE 106 (Electricity and Magnetism) and SE 380 (Introduction to Feedback Control) were the most disliked courses. Most of the disliked courses were core courses, although some of them were also Linkage Electives (SMF 101, SPCOMM 100, SPCOMM 223)

Least favourite mandatory courses

Largest Workload

Among core courses, SE 350 (Operating Systems) and CS 343 (Concurrent and Parallel Programming) were the courses with the largest workload. Among electives CS 444 (Compiler Construction) was the course with the largest workload. Although these courses involved a high workload, most of them were also popular among respondents.

Course with the largest workload

Overloading

Overloading & Academic Terms

28 respondents overloaded at some point during their academic degree. The 4A and 3A terms are the most popular term to overload. The SE curriculum encourages students to overload in their 4A term to have a lighter term in 4B, which can be busy with graduation traditions and FYDP work on top of courses.

Overloading vs Academic Terms

Overloading Reasons

The most popular reasons to overload were to have a lighter term and to take more electives than prescribed.
